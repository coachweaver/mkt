Getting Started

Club Proposal
& Flyer Assignment

Your Goal: Design a new school club from scratch. You'll write a formal proposal to administration and create a promotional flyer for your club's interest meeting.

Two Graded Deliverables

DeliverableDescriptionGraded?
Proposal PDF One-page, black & white document in MLA format Graded
Club Flyer PDF Portrait orientation promotional flyer designed in Canva Graded

Assignment Schedule

Day 1 75 minutes
  1. Brainstorm your club concept (10–15 min)
  2. Draft your proposal in Google Docs (25–30 min)
  3. Plan your flyer design concept (10 min)
  4. Begin flyer design in Canva (20–25 min)
Day 2 75 minutes
  1. Finish and polish your flyer (60 min)
  2. Export both deliverables as PDFs (10 min)
  3. Submit to Google Classroom (5 min)

File Naming Convention

Required Format — [lastName firstName] Document Name.pdf
Smith John Proposal.pdf Smith John Club Flyer.pdf

Grading System

This assignment uses binary grading: Acceptable or Unacceptable. Your work either meets every required criterion or it does not — no partial points. Use the interactive checklists on the Proposal and Flyer pages to verify your work before submitting.

Submission Steps

  1. Go to Google Classroom and open the assignment.
  2. Click Add or Create → File.
  3. Upload both PDF files (Proposal + Flyer).
  4. Verify both files are attached and correctly named.
  5. Click Turn In and confirm.
Before you submit: Open each PDF and confirm it looks correct. Once submitted, you cannot edit unless you click "Unsubmit."

Deliverable 1

Proposal Guidelines

Audience: You are writing to Mr. Dillard to convince school administration your club should be approved. Use professional, formal language throughout.
Critical Requirements
  • Maximum 1 page — do not go over
  • Black and white only — no colors or images
  • MLA format required (see below)
  • Written in Google Docs, exported as PDF

MLA Formatting

Header — top-left corner

Your Name
Mr. Dillard
3A Marketing
30 March 2026

ElementRequirement
FontTimes New Roman, 12pt
SpacingDouble-spaced throughout
Margins1 inch on all sides
TitleCentered, same font and size as body — not bold or underlined
Page NumberTop-right corner with your last name (e.g., "Smith 1")

Required Sections

1. Purpose Statement

2–4 sentences explaining what your club does and why it should exist. Answer: What is the focus? What need does it serve? What value does it give members?

Example

The Creative Writing Club provides students with a supportive community to develop their writing skills across multiple genres. Members will workshop their creative pieces, participate in writing challenges, and prepare submissions for literary competitions. This club addresses the need for creative outlets and helps students build portfolios for college applications.

2. Meeting Schedule & Target Audience

Include: meeting frequency, duration, proposed day/time, and who the club is for.

Example

The Creative Writing Club will meet every Tuesday during lunch (30 minutes) in Room 204. Meetings are open to students in grades 9–12 of all skill levels, from beginners to advanced writers preparing for college creative writing programs.

3. Required Resources & Leadership Structure

Include: space needs, equipment/budget, leadership roles, and faculty advisor status.

Example

The club requires a classroom with moveable seating to facilitate small group workshops. Initial supplies include printer paper and writing prompts (estimated $30/semester). Leadership will consist of a President, Vice President, and Workshop Coordinator elected by members. Ms. Johnson has expressed interest in serving as faculty advisor.

Writing Tips

Do

  • Use formal, professional language
  • Be specific — give real details
  • Show you've thought through logistics
  • Keep every sentence purposeful
  • Focus on what the club will do

Don't

  • Use slang or casual language
  • Make vague, general statements
  • Go over one page
  • Add colors, images, or decorations
  • Leave spelling or grammar errors

Deliverable 2

Flyer Design Guide

Tool: Design your flyer in Canva. Use portrait orientation (8.5" × 11"). Export as PDF Standard when finished.

Quick Design Reference

Color — Pick 2–3 that match your club's identity
Red — Energy, urgency
Blue — Trust, calm
Yellow — Optimism, creativity
Green — Growth, balance
Purple — Imagination, wisdom
Pink — Playfulness, care

Contrast rule: Dark text on light backgrounds, or light text on dark backgrounds. Never light-on-light or dark-on-dark.

Fonts: Use one bold heading font + one clean body font + one accent/fun font. Never exceed 3 fonts. Never pair two decorative fonts together.

Required Flyer Elements

Every element below must appear on your flyer.

ElementWhat It Means
Club NameLarge and prominent — readable from across the room
Purpose StatementOne sentence explaining what the club is about
Meeting DateSpecific date of the interest meeting
Meeting TimeStart time clearly stated
Meeting LocationRoom number or building
Call to Action (CTA)Tell people exactly what to do next
Eye-Catching ElementOne creative thing that makes your flyer stand out

Advertising Principles

1. Visual Hierarchy

Guide the viewer's eye in order of importance. Club name and main visual are largest. Meeting details are medium. Fine print and CTA are smallest — but still readable.

2. The 3-Second Rule

A person walking past your flyer should understand what it is in 3 seconds. If you need more than 15 words to explain your club, simplify it.

3. White Space is Your Friend

Don't fill every inch of the page. Empty space makes important info stand out and makes your design look professional. Cramped designs are hard to read.

4. Call to Action Examples

  • "Scan to sign up!"
  • "See you there!"
  • "Join us Monday!"
  • "Follow @clubhandle for updates"
  • "Text JOIN to 12345"

5. Eye-Catching Element Ideas

Your flyer needs one thing that makes it stand out from every other flyer on the board. Consider: diagonal or circular text layouts, a bold graphic that dominates the page, a QR code, creative typography used as art, a strong background texture or pattern, a color-block design, or a visual pun related to your club's theme.

Design rule: Every creative choice should serve your message, not distract from it. If it doesn't help someone understand your club faster, reconsider it.

Common Mistakes

Do

  • Make club name huge — readable from 10 feet away
  • Use high-quality images — no pixelation
  • Align elements to create clean edges
  • Leave generous margins (0.5" min on all sides)
  • Proofread everything at least twice

Don't

  • Use more than 3 fonts
  • Write long paragraphs — use bullets or short phrases
  • Center-align every single element
  • Use clipart or blurry stock photos
  • Make any text smaller than 10pt

Exporting from Canva

  1. Click Share in the top-right corner of Canva.
  2. Select Download.
  3. Choose PDF Standard from the file type dropdown.
  4. Click Download.
  5. Rename the file using the correct format (e.g., Smith John Club Flyer.pdf).
